# Thought Garden

A personal bilingual digital garden (English + French) focused on AI, LLMs, data science, and technology. Content is non-linear, grows over time, and reflects the author's thinking — not a sequential publication feed.

## Concepts

**Digital garden**:
A personal knowledge space that grows and evolves over time, reflecting the author's thinking and connections between ideas — as opposed to a chronological blog. Articles are interconnected nodes, not sequential entries.
_Avoid_: Blog, publication

## Language

**Article**:
A self-contained piece of writing published on the blog.
_Avoid_: Post, entry

**Translation**:
A derived language variant of an article, authored after and based on the original. Each article has at most one translation. English is the default authoring language, so the typical direction is EN→FR; FR→EN translations exist but are exceptional.
_Avoid_: Version, copy

**Tag**:
A fine-grained label applied to an article to describe its specific subject. Always lowercase kebab-case (e.g. `llm`, `paper-reading`) and always written in English regardless of the article's language.
_Avoid_: Label, keyword

**Category**:
A broad topic area grouping articles at a higher level than tags (e.g. "AI", "Productivity").
_Avoid_: Section, topic

**Page**:
A static, evergreen piece of content that describes the author or advertises their work — categorically distinct from articles. Not part of the garden's knowledge content.
_Avoid_: Article, post

**Source note**:
A note in the author's personal Zettelkasten vault (distilled Permanent Notes, sourced Literature notes, raw Fleeting notes). Source material that feeds an article — the author's own thinking on a topic — but categorically not an article itself, the same way a Page is not an article. Consulted when drafting; never published directly.
_Avoid_: Article, draft

## Example dialogue

> "Should I add a French translation for the new LLM article?"
> "Yes — write it as a translation of the English original, add the auto-translation notice at the top, keep all tags in English."

> "Where should the CLI tools article live?"
> "In the garden as an article, not a page. Pages are only for author-facing content like work-with-me."

> "Should we tag the SkillsRL review with 'paper-reading' or 'PaperReading'?"
> "Tags are lowercase kebab-case by convention — use 'paper-reading'."
